The Monarch Butterfly

The monarch butterfly is one of the most well-known insects in North America. It is famous for its bright orange and black wings, which make it easy to recognize. Every year, millions of monarch butterflies travel from Canada and the United States all the way to the forests of central Mexico. This journey can be more than four thousand kilometers long, making it one of the longest migrations of any insect in the world.

Scientists have been studying how monarch butterflies find their way during migration. Research has shown that they use the position of the sun as a kind of compass to keep moving in the right direction. Even on cloudy days, they can sense the sun's position using special cells in their eyes. This ability allows them to travel accurately without getting lost, even over such a long distance.

When monarch butterflies arrive in Mexico, they gather in large groups on certain trees in the mountains. Millions of butterflies rest on the branches together, which keeps them warm during the cold winter months. Local people in Mexico have known about this gathering for many generations, and today it has become an important part of their culture. Tourists from around the world also visit the area every year to see this amazing natural event.

In recent years, the number of monarch butterflies has been decreasing. The main reasons are the loss of milkweed plants, which monarchs need to lay their eggs, and changes in weather patterns caused by climate change. Several organizations are now working to protect both the butterflies and their habitat. They are planting more milkweed in parks and gardens, and encouraging people to reduce the use of chemicals that harm the plants the butterflies depend on.
